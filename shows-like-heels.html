Shows Like Heels: Entertaining Series Recommendations

In Starz’s “Heels,” unwavering ambition turns lives. The story of two brothers who also happen to be each other’s competitors is followed in the drama, which centres on the tight-knit community of a small Georgian town. One plays the evil, or “heel,” while the other plays the hero, or “face.” The two brothers play out planned bouts and keep everyone in the dark in an effort to uphold their late father’s legacy and compete for national exposure. The show, which was created by Michael Waldron and Mike O’Malley, explores a number of conundrums and the irrational effects of popularity, rivalry, and contempt.

Stephen Amell, Alexander Ludwig, Alison Luff, Mary McCormack, Kelli Berglund, Allen Maldonado, and James Harrison all deliver strong performances in the drama television series. ‘Heels’ explores not only the all-consuming fixation with winning the top rank but also the absurdity of a family narrative as two brothers compete against one another at every turn. So, if you were equally captivated by the struggle between siblings that results in a fierce rivalry, here is a list of related book suggestions. Several of these programmes, including “Heels,” are available to stream on Netflix, Hulu, or Amazon Prime.

Bloodhounds (2023)

This action-packed series likewise has two male characters on a mission, similar to Jake and Ace. But unlike Jack and Ace, Kim Gun-woo and Hong Woo-Jin don’t have a stronger rivalry. Instead, the team aids those who have fallen under the threatening influence of loan sharks. Private lending enterprises boom and make good profits during the global financial crisis when the epidemic strikes and people start losing their jobs.

The plot centres on two former Marine boxers who team together to defeat a loan shark who wants to obliterate small businesses. “Bloodhounds,” starring Woo Do-Hwan and Lee Sang-Yi, has drama, action, and a compelling plot. So, if the drama and internal strife in “Heels” had you spellbound, you’ll find this duo’s quest for justice to be just as compelling.

Blue Demon (2016-2017)

‘Blue Demon’ tells the tale of the illustrious Mexican wrestler known by his ring name Blue Demon and is another programme that explores the life of heritage. The series, which is quickly making its way to success, recounts the path of the legend who resisted being revealed in truth. Alejandro Munoz had never disclosed his face to the world, but the series follows his life’s journey and how he became famous.

‘Blue Demon’ also draws on the awe of athletics and stars Tenoch Huerta, Ana Brenda Contreras, Ianis Guerrero, and Alejandro de Marino. ‘Blue Demon’ focuses on the path of a man who never revealed his true personality to the public, just like Jack and Ace write their bouts and refuse to tell the truth. Therefore, if you were captivated by the story of two brothers competing to carry on a legacy, you will also be intrigued by the tale of a guy on a mission to establish his reputation.

Fight For My Way (2017)

In this Korean drama, the overwhelming strength of ambition and desire is highlighted. The drama series, starring Park Seo-Joon and Kim Ji-Won, centres on a former taekwondo competitor whose career is cut short owing to a traumatic past. Now, an unnamed mixed martial artist by the name of Ko Dong Man seeks to triumph over the many obstacles in his path and reach the top.

Ko is drawn to his boyhood best buddy as he tries to get around the obstacles in his way. ‘Fight for My Way’ showcases the captivating and engrossing impact of a person’s aspirations via romance, drama, and dreams. So, if Jack and Ace’s relentless pursuit of victory captured your attention, this play will present you with a number of topics that will.

Friday Night Lights (2006-2011)

The Dillon Panthers, a highly regarded high school football team, and their head coach Eric Taylor are the subjects of the drama. The drama delves into the complex socioeconomic concerns that plague small towns and is also based in a fictional tiny town with a tight-knit community in rural West Texas. The pupils are not only grappling with change, but they are also experiencing racism.

The programme depicts a number of problems faced by the young athletes in relation to social issues including poverty and lack of status. The appropriate programme to watch after “Heels” is “Friday Night Lights,” which likewise showcases the undeniable draw of football in a Texan town and the overwhelming impact of athletics in a tiny town.

GLOW (2017-2019)

In contrast to the acrimonious rivalry that takes the form of mutual sabotage in “Heels,” “GLOW” centres on a group of female wrestlers who competed on the syndicated Gorgeous Ladies of Wrestling circuit in the 1980s. Each participant in the circuit contributes something special, but it is not their prowess in the ring that unites them.

Instead, the programme focuses on the absurd ways in which the ladies confront injustice and forge ahead in life. The 1980s drama “GLOW” depicts women’s ascent in environments created by and for males. Drama and comedy inherently follow when the women band together to tip the scales in their favour. The programme was created by Liz Flahive and Carly Mensch and stars Britney Young, Betty Gilpin, Marc Maron, Sydelle Noel, and Alison Brie. ‘GLOW’ shows the great enchantment of the sport in a similar way to ‘Heels,’ making it the ideal TV to binge after!

Kingdom (2014-2017)

‘Kingdom’ chronicles the narrative of a former MMA fighter, his two sons, and his fiancée as they co-own a fighting academy in Los Angeles. This show also focuses on turbulent family relations. However, when a troublesome fighter from their past returns to train at their facility, things swiftly go south. The sitcom, which was created by Byron Balasco, stars Frank Grillo, Kiele Sanchez, Matt Lauria, and Jonathan Tucker. The perfect programme to watch next is “Kingdom,” which, like Jack and Ace’s problematic secrets, has a gripping tale that alludes to the struggles people have as a result of failed familial relationships.

One Tree Hill (2003-2012)

The story of two half-brothers Nathan and Lucas Scott, who are forced into competition against their will, is told in “One Tree Hill,” another television series that centres on the vitality of sports in a small community. While Lucas is blessed with innate talent and continues to hone it without any other assistance, Nathan grows up with the proper upbringing and inevitably develops into a budding basketball great.

When the two brothers come into contact, a ferocious rivalry starts and slowly develops into something else. ‘One Tree Hill’ and ‘Heels’ have significant thematic similarities and both feature equal amounts of drama, romance, and heartbreak. ‘One Tree Hill’, starring James Lafferty, Chad Michael Murray, Bethany Joy Lenz, Sophia Bush, and Hilary Burton, is based on the same sports and rivalry tale that made ‘Heels’ so compelling.

Young Rock (2021-2023)

Even though it’s a sitcom, “Young Rock” chronicles the crucial years in Dwayne “The Rock” Johnson’s youth that ultimately helped him establish himself as a well-known professional wrestler. The struggles he went through as a young man are nevertheless depicted in the series, despite the fact that it may not depict the struggles of two brothers. The story has various important topics, from dealing with bullying to figuring out who he is. Therefore, despite the fact that Dwayne’s opponent wasn’t a relative, the story of the aspiring star still had many interesting subplots.
