Tami Gingold Used to be A Popular Actress and Comedian

However, Tami Gingold is mostly remembered as an actress. She did some acting in Israel and a few in Hollywood after her migration. However, Tami Gingold didn’t gain much fame as an actress and only took up supporting actress roles.

Her entire filmography comprises only four movies: Anashim BeMil in 1984 as a random sergeant, The 17th Bride in 1985, House Committee Rivalry the following year in 1986 as Yardena Shoresh, and finally, Morning Star in 1980. Of all these movies, her best-known one was The 17th Bride.

The 17th Bride is a war drama set in the Czechoslovakian Republic that focuses on a 30-year-old Jewish woman who was forced to marry a man she didn’t like to avoid being sent to a war camp. Ultimately, the woman (Lisa Hartman) developed a mental illness due to the immense stress caused by WWII.

The movie featured actors like Lisa Hartman, who played Liza, Rosemary Leach, who played Esther, and Gingold, who played a passing character named Magda. The other movies in Gingold’s credits were made between 1980 and 1986, which were Tami’s most active on-screen years.

The actress then decided to make one last career change, this time to become a therapist. Very little is known about Tami Gingold’s therapy practice, but she is licensed and qualified to practice in the United States.
